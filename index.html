Problem Statement & Background

E-banking in rural India faces a fundamental paradox — the technology exists, but the knowledge and infrastructure to use it effectively do not. Rural customers in areas like Kappur and Pattithara Panchayat are cut off from the full benefits of digital banking due to limited digital literacy, poor internet connectivity, and persistent security anxieties.

This research investigates how e-banking services impact customer satisfaction among rural residents, uncovering the real barriers that prevent financial inclusion from becoming a reality on the ground.

2

Research Objectives

Assess awareness and adoption levels of e-banking among rural individuals
Evaluate the benefits e-banking provides for rural customers
Identify key challenges and barriers to e-banking adoption in rural areas
Provide actionable recommendations to support rural e-banking growth
3

Research Methodology

A descriptive research design was adopted. Primary data was collected through a structured questionnaire administered to 59 e-banking consumers across Kappur and Pattithara Panchayath using convenient sampling. Analysis tools included Ranking and Likert scale methods.

Key Findings

Adoption DriverCustomers primarily adopt e-banking for safety and security — not just convenience or cost.
Highest SatisfactionMoney transfer and balance checking receive the highest satisfaction scores among rural users.
Fraud Awareness83% of respondents are aware of hacking risks; 81.4% aware of suspicious messages requesting bank details.
Digital Device88.1% access e-banking via mobile phone — desktop/laptop usage is minimal in rural areas.
Bank PreferenceSBI dominates rural e-banking at 45.8%, followed by Canara Bank and rural cooperative banks.
Loan GapOnly 3.4% use e-banking for applying loans — a significant untapped opportunity for rural credit access.
8

What Customers Want Improved

🔒 Improve Security Features (40) 🎧 Better Customer Support (39) 🖥️ More User-Friendly UI (28) 🌐 More Language Support (23) 💳 More Payment Options (23)
9

Recommendations

Banks must conduct digital literacy workshops and awareness programs in rural panchayats
Government investment in rural digital infrastructure is essential for sustained adoption
Banks should prioritise security enhancements — the top customer concern
Regular customer experience surveys should guide iterative service improvements
Server maintenance schedules must be planned to avoid disruption during peak usage
Multi-language support should be expanded to serve non-English speaking rural users
10

Conclusion

E-banking holds transformative potential for rural India — enabling customers to transfer money, check balances, and access financial services without visiting a branch. However, realising this potential requires targeted action on digital literacy, security infrastructure, and customer support. This study found that rural customers are willing adopters when trust is established. The path to full financial inclusion in rural areas runs directly through improved e-banking accessibility, better security systems, and sustained awareness campaigns by banks and government alike.
